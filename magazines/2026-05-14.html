The faculty voted Monday to require universal proctoring for all in-person examinations in response to mounting concerns over AI-assisted cheating and declining student reporting of Honor Code violations. The policy, which takes effect July 1, preserves the student-run Honor Committee system while requiring instructors to serve as witnesses. All in-person examinations at Princeton will be proctored starting July 1, representing the most significant change to the honor system since it was established in 1893.

The faculty passed a proposal requiring instructor supervision at Monday’s faculty meeting, with one opposing vote.
