Blog Games Projects Dominic Szablewski, @phoboslab — Monday, May 4th 2026 Additive Blending on the Nintendo 64 Did you ever wonder why explosions and other effects looked so much cooler on the original PlayStation than they did on the Nintendo 64? The reason is additive blending! Or rather, in the N64 case, the lack thereof. While the N64 actually did support additive blending, it was practically unusable.

The PSX supports 4 different blend modes (in addition to just overwriting pixels) to control how sprites and geometry are mixed into the existing frame buffer: 0: (src + dst) / 2 1: src + dst 2: dst - src 3: dst + src/4 The one you see here in Silent Bomber is conceptually the simplest one: src + dst . That is, colors are just added to the existing ones in the frame buffer.
